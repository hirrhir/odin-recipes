Shiro

Shiro is one of Ethiopia's most beloved dishes, a rich, flavorful stew made primarily from chickpea flour (shiro). It's simple, hearty, and usually eaten with injera.

Ingredients

  • Shiro powder
    • Chickpea flour mixed with spice and berbere - 1 and 1/2 cups
    • If you only have plain chickpea flour, you'll need to add berbere, garlic and other spices (or only berebere)
  • 1-2 tablespoons berbere spice (depending on your spice preference)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped or blended)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium tomato (finely chopped or blended)
  • 3 tablespoons spiced butter (nitre kibbeh) optional (personally prefers without butter)
  • 1 litre water
  • salt

Step's with (chickpea flour mixed with spice and berebere)

  1. Prepare the base
    1. Heat a pot on medium heat.
    2. Add the choped onions without oil first. stir continuously until they sweat down or loose water (about 5-7 minutes).
    3. Add oil and cook until onions turn slightly brown.
    4. Add tomato cook for (about 20-30 minutes).
  2. Add flavour
    1. Add garlic in the middle of the 20-30 min of tomato cooking.
  3. Build the stew
    1. pour 1 litre water and let cook for (10-15 minutes).
    2. Add salt.
    3. Add shiro flour stirring while adding to avoid lumps.
    4. Then let cook for 10 minutes.
  4. finish
    1. Add spiced butter (optional).
    2. On low heat let simmer for about 20 minutes.
  5. Serving
    • Traditionally served hot with injera.
    • you can garnish with fresh green chili.
    • Eat by tearing off a piece of injera and scooping up the shiro.

Step's with (chickpea flour without berbere)

  1. Prepare the base
    1. Heat a pot on medium heat.
    2. Add the choped onions without oil first. stir continuously until they sweat down or loose water (about 5-7 minutes).
    3. Add oil and cook until onions turn slightly brown.
    4. Add berbere spice and stir well for about 5-7 minutes.
    5. Add garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
    6. Add tomato and cook for about 5 minutes.
  2. Build the stew
    1. pour 1 litre water and let cook for (10-15 minutes).
    2. Add salt.
    3. Add shiro flour stirring while adding to avoid lumps.
    4. Then let cook for 10 minutes.
  3. finish
    1. Add spiced butter (optional).
    2. On low heat let simmer for about 20 minutes.
  4. Serving
    • Traditionally served hot with injera.
    • you can garnish with fresh green chili.
    • Eat by tearing off a piece of injera and scooping up the shiro.

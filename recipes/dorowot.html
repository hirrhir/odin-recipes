DORO WOT

DoroWot is one of Ethiopia's most famous dishes. A rich, spicy, chicken stew made with berbere, onions, and clarified spiced butter (yetenetere kibbeh). It is often served with injera.

Ingredients

  • 3 Kilograms of onion
  • 2 Cups of food oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 cups berbere
  • 1 and 1/2 cups spiced butter (yetenetere kibbeh)
  • 1/2 tablespoon black cardamom()
  • Mekelesha()
  • 2 tablespoon salt
  • 1 Whole chicken
  • 12 hard boiled eggs

Step's

  1. Prepare the chicken
    1. Wash the chicken pieces thoroughly
    2. Rub them with lemon juice and a salt. Let them sit for about 15 minutes.
    3. Rinse again with water and set aside
  2. Caramelize the onions
    1. In a large heavy pot, add the chopped onions without oil
    2. Cook on medium heat,stirring continuously,until the onions soften up and loose all their water
  3. Adding of berbere, making (kulet)
    1. Once it starts turning brown add the 2 cups of oil
    2. Once onions are browned add the 2 cups of berbere and let cook adding small amout of hot water
    3. Add a well grinded garlic
    4. This creates a thick red paste
  4. Add the prepared chicken
    1. Add the chicken pieces to the sauce and let cook for 1 hour
    2. Stir well to coat each piece with the spicy mixture
  5. Add water/spiced butter/some spice
    1. pour in about 1 liter water
    2. stir in the spiced butter
    3. season with salt and black cardamom
    4. simmer for 20 minutes,until the sauce is thick and rich
  6. Add the boiled eggs
    1. Gently poke each hard-boiled egg eith fork (so they absorb flavor)
    2. Add them into the stew during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
    3. Simmer until eggs are coated in sauce
  7. Serve hot with injera.
